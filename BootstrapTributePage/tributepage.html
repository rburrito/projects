My All-Time Favorite Sci-Fi and Fantasy Shows


List of favorite sci-fi and fantasy shows:

  1. Buffy the Vampire Slayer
    Although Buffy came out almost 20 years ago, it remains one of the most progressive shows of its time. It provided a good mix of action, romance, comedy, and mystery. I admit that some seasons are much better than others, but Joss Whedon managed to create a satisfactory ending to the final season. Click here to read Buffy's Wiki page.

  2. Firefly
    I had a hard time getting into the show until the end of the second episode. By the time I got to "Our Mrs. Reynolds", I was completely hooked and had to go back to rewatch all the witty lines I missed in the first few episodes. I definitely enjoyed watching Gina Torres, Summer Glau, and Nathan Fillion. Unfortunately, it ended with only 11 episodes. Click here to learn more on the Firefly Wiki.

  3. Charmed
    Although the last few seasons left more to be desired, I really enjoyed the first four seasons, especially the beginning of Piper and Leo's romance. Season five is still tolerable, but the inconsistencies of the later seasons were a little annoying. Read the Charmed Wiki for more.

  4. Fringe
    Fringe brings my imagination to life as the parallel world reminds me just a bit of The Man in the High Castle. I really enjoyed the what-ifs and the slight character changes brought on by the different circumstances each character experiences. I loved the entire cast, but the standout actor is clearly John Noble. Find out more about the characters on the Fringe Wiki.

  5. Alphas
    Alphas is what Heroes should have been. It took me some time, but I grew to love the characters as the season went on. I think I was more drawn to the science-y aspect of this show. Unfortunately, this show was canceled right when it found its momentum. Click here to go to the Alphas Wiki.

  6. Terminator: The Sarah Connor Chronicles
    To be honest, I started watching this show because Summer Glau was in it. While I enjoyed her performance as Cameron Phillips, John Connor's most trusted reprogrammed Terminator, I was blown away by Garrett Dillahunt as Cromartie and later as John Henry. I could not believe he was the goofy grandfather in Raising Hope. Of course, the show did not get enough viewers and was prematurely canceled. Read more on the Sarah Connor Chronicles Wiki.

  7. Orphan Black
    This is a relatively new series compared to the rest of the shows on this list. Tatiana Maslany plays countless characters in this thrilling clone conspiracy. I am continually amazed by her acting chops and the range of characters she is required to play. The show does have a ridiculously convoluted storyline making it difficult to keep track of all the events that happened. However, I continue to look forward to every season because I have already fallen in love with the characters to turn back now. For more information, visit the Orphan Black Wiki.
